# Akutes Aortensyndrom
"Acute Aortic Syndrome" refers to the typical **clinical presentation** associated most commonly with dissection (i.e. tearing chest pain). It is also used as an umbrella term for the three pathologies that most commonly lead to this clinical presentation:

- **Aortic Dissection** (this case)
- **Acute intramural hematoma** - thought related to ruptured vaso-vasorum in the wall. Appearance on CT is hyperattenuation in the wall on NON-CONTRAST acquisition. This is why **all dissection protocols should have a non-contrast acquisition** first!
- **Penetrating Atherosclerotic ulcer** - ulceration into atheroma that extends beyond the intima, resulting in acute hematoma in the wall (also hyperattenuating in the wall) or eventually a dissection

All three of these conditions are managed similarly, and all classified similarly.

# Standford A vs. B
**Type A** involves the **Ascending Aorta**.
- Many resources will tell you this is "proximal to the left subclavian", but this is incorrect!
- The ascending aorta is **proximal to the brachiocephalic!**

**Type B** was initially described to involve the descending aorta, but includes anything that is not a type A (i.e. **distal to brachiocephalic**). It can be broken down further:
- **Type B dissection** (descending aorta, distal to subclavian artery)
- **Type B with arch involvement** (involvement proximal to subclavian, but not involving ascending -- still technically type B).
#### **Management:**
Classic teaching is that Type **A is generally treated** **surgically**. Type **B generally medically** (i.e. BP control).

# Komplikationen
**Type A:**
- **Dissection into coronary artery** (right more common than left) - can result in MI
- **Rupture into pericardium** - resulting in **hemopericardium** +/- findings of **tamponade** (similar to right heart strain on CT)

**General:**
- End organ ischemia (i.e. abdominal organs, stroke, etc)
- Aortic rupture

# Echtes vs. falsches Lumen
If more proximal normal vessel/aortic root **communicates directly** with one of the two lumens -- then that is the true lumen by definition. If able to identify this directly then you know which is true and which is false. If unable, the following are helpful to know.

**True lumen:**
- **[Contiguous with the aortic root](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30743&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30773&ww=871&wc=202&an=true)** (discussed above)
- **Outer wall calcifications** (which are in the intima) - _not present here, with no aortic calcifications_
- **[Often smaller of the two](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30432&ww=637&wc=160&an=true)**(compressed by higher pressure in the false lumen)
- **Often feeds the celiac, SMA, [right renal](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf3048d&ww=637&wc=160&an=true) artery**. _Here, the dissection itself extends into the other branches including the SMA._

**False Lumen:**
- **Often [larger](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30432&ww=637&wc=160&an=true)**[](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30432&ww=637&wc=160&an=true)(higher pressure)
- **Often lower attenuation** (delayed opacification), or may be thrombosed/less well opacified
- **Often left aspect of descending aorta, [feeding left renal artery](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf30487&ww=700&wc=267&an=true)**
- **[Beak sign](https://radiopaedia.org/articles/beak-sign-arterial-dissection)** - acute angle of false lumen surrounding the true
- **[Often surrounds the true lumen](https://app.navigatingradiology.com/course-types/on-call-preparation/courses/ct-chest-and-ctpa/cases/14039/diagnosis?s=63e6ae871315cc5dccf303f2&i=63e6ae871315cc5dccf3041d&ww=700&wc=267&an=true)** in type A
- Higher risk of rupture
